Examples

  • 1. Paper/Cardboard - Can be recycled by local recycling centers. Flatten cardboard boxes to save space.
  • 2. Plastics - Separate into categories like PET, HDPE, etc., to ensure proper recycling.
  • 3. Glass - Rinse containers before recycling to remove any residues.
  • 4. Aluminum - Easily recyclable; check with local centers for recycling options.
  • 5. Batteries - Dispose of properly; many stores offer battery recycling programs.
  • 6. Electronics - Recycle old electronics to recover valuable metals and prevent environmental damage.
  • 7. Food - Compost organic waste to reduce landfill and create nutrient-rich soil.
  • 8. Lawn Materials - Grass clippings and leaves can be composted or used as mulch.
  • 9. Used Oil - Recycle used motor oil at designated drop-off locations.
  • 10. Household Hazardous Waste - Dispose of items like paints, cleaners, and pesticides at hazardous waste facilities.
  • 11. Tires - Recycle tires into various products or through tire recycling programs.
  • 12. Metal - Recycle scrap metal to conserve natural resources and reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
  • 13. Miscellaneous - Items like clothing, furniture, and toys can often be donated or repurposed.
